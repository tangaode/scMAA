Requirements:
- Pick one strong, feasible hypothesis.
- Use the research brief and validation summary to avoid unsupported claims.
- The first notebook step must produce evidence, not generic setup.
- Prefer RNA-ATAC questions such as paired state differences, concordant marker programs, accessibility support for RNA-defined states, or tissue-stratified regulatory shifts.
- If the dataset has many shared RNA-ATAC observation names, assume paired multiome analysis is feasible unless the validation summary says otherwise.
- If gene activity is available, favor a `joint` hypothesis that can be tested with both expression and ATAC support.
- Do not default to `rna_only` when the same question can be framed as a stronger RNA-ATAC joint analysis.
- The first notebook step should use RNA and ATAC evidence together whenever the hypothesis is about genes, markers, states, or regulatory programs.
